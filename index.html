Portfolio

TEL Off-Grid

CONTEXT: Technology Exchange Lab is an NGO focused on creating a curated database of "solutions to problems of poverty." They enlisted me as part of a mobile app development team whose goal was to make their database available to more people in more places. As a team our first step was to put together a user profile and a set of user requirements. Through our research we learned two important lessons. First, our app needed a clear and simple user interface. This would ensure that everyone could use it, even those with little prior technological experience. Second, the application needed to function offline so that it would be usable anywhere in the world.

RESULTS: The project was a success, and is currently available on the Google Play Store. Next steps include the development of an iOS app and creating a second iteration following more user research.

Kio Kit Statistics Portal

CONTEXT: The Kio Kit is an educational solution that can turn any classroom into a high tech learning space. This past summer, BRCK (the company behind the Kio Kit) began implementing a user account system with the purpose of collecting data to show to students, teachers, and administrators. My task was to develop a preliminary information architecture and user interface for displaying this data. I first identified 6 user groups who would need different information (students, teachers, school administrators, content providers, BRCK employees, and government officials). My next step was to interview users that fell into these categories to determine the needs of each group. Finally, I took what I had learned and developed a set of wireframes.

RESULTS: Given the complex nature of this project, my job was just to come up with a preliminary set of designs. Once finished, I presented my work to BRCK's UX lead who used my research and design as the basis for the final product.

BRCK.org Redesign

CONTEXT: BRCK.org was looking for a website redesign to increase loyalty among visitors and drive traffick to a new donation page. Through user research we discovered that, when we told BRCK.org's story clearly and concisely through text, images, and video, people felt more connected to the organization and their mission statement.

RESULTS: While our solution has not yet been implemented, it has been approved by the design team at BRCK.org, and will be implemented over the next 6 months as time permits.
